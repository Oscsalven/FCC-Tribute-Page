Fernando Valenuzela

A look into the life and career of Mexico's greatest pitcher

Introducing Fernando

  • 1960 - Fernando was born on november 1st in Etchohuaqula, Sonora
  • 1977 - Signs his first professional contract with Cafeteros de Tepic.
  • 1979 - Fernando signs with Angeles de Puebla. He is drafted to Yucantan, with los Leones later on that year.
  • Summer, 1979 - Signs a contract with the LA Dodgers, pitching with Double-A team San Antonio Dodgers
  • 1980 - Near the end of the mayor league season, Fernando gets called to pitch against the Houston Astros
  • 1981 - Fernando pitches for the entire opening day game versus the Houston Astros, winning 2-0. Fernandomania was born.
  • 1986 - He made history in the All-Star Game, taking out five batters in a row.
  • 1991 - He left the Dodgers and returned to Mexico, signing with the Charros de Jalisco.
  • 1996 - Fernando made a solid return to the MLB, with the San Diego Padres
  • 1997 - Fernando retires from the major league after ending the season with the St. Louis Cardinals
  • 2004 - Mayos de Navojoa, from the Mexican Pacific League, signs Fernando. He would later go to Hermosillo and finally Mexicalli
  • 2006 - Fernando plays his last professional game of his career, with the Aguilas de Mexicalli.
  • 2017 - He acquires the Tigres de Quintana Roo, Mexico's second most succesful team.
